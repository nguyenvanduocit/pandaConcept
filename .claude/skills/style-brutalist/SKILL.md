# Brutalist Interior Design

---

## Philosophy
*Béton brut* — raw concrete. Brutalism (1950s–1970s) was an architectural and social movement: buildings for the people, honest about their materials, refusing decorative concealment. As interior design, Brutalism is the most challenging aesthetic — it asks you to find beauty in weight, mass, austerity, and the geometry of necessity. The 2024 revival (trending in *US News & World Report* as 2024's hot interior design trend) brings warmth and lived-in quality to the ideology without diluting its convictions.

---

## Core Characteristics
1. Exposed, unpainted concrete — board-marked texture visible
2. Severe geometric forms — rectangles, triangles, acute angles
3. Massive, heavy furniture — low to ground, slab-like
4. Monochromatic palette dominated by grey, black, concrete
5. No applied ornament — beauty from mass, proportion, material alone
6. Oversized scale — small things look wrong; everything must be architectural
7. Industrial and utilitarian fixtures treated as design
8. Concrete walls, floors, and ceilings creating total material envelope
9. Limited natural material warmth (contrast makes it work)
10. Structural honesty — form reveals exactly how it was made
11. Cantilevered and floating elements — demonstrating engineering
12. Window slits or narrow reveals creating dramatic light shafts

## Materials

| Material | Specifics | Application |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Board-formed concrete | In-situ pour with visible board texture | Walls, floors, ceilings, furniture table bases |
| Poured concrete (polished) | Mechanically finished smooth slab | Floors, bathroom surfaces |
| Corten steel (weathering steel) | Self-rusting orange-brown patina | Accents, furniture, screens |
| Rough-cut black granite | Unpolished dark stone | Counters, feature elements |
| Exposed concrete block (CMU) | Standard masonry unit, unplastered | Internal walls |
| Matte black steel rebar | Industrial reinforcement bar | Decorative sculpture, furniture frames |
| Dark-stained oak | Ebonized or deep-stained hardwood | Flooring contrast, primary warming element |
| Unfinished plywood (Baltic birch) | Raw void-free plywood | Storage, shelving |
| Black rubber flooring | Industrial vulcanized rubber | Floors — matte, tactile |
| Velvet (dark toned) | Deep-pile woven fabric | Softening upholstery on heavy frames |
| Polished zinc | Mirror-finished zinc sheet | Countertops, table surfaces |
| Poured resin (black or charcoal) | Cast polymer in dark tone | Bespoke table tops, sinks |

## Signature Furniture
- Concrete slab dining table on concrete pedestal base
- Low-slung leather sofa with exposed concrete/steel frame
- Brutalist bookshelf — angular, asymmetric, raw plywood
- Concrete planter (geometric, oversized)
- Steel-frame bed with concrete headboard panel
- Cubo (cube) stool in solid concrete

---

## Lighting
- Bare industrial bulb (opaline or frosted globe) in minimal socket
- Concrete pendant light (poured concrete shade)
- Light shafts from narrow slot windows creating chiaroscuro
- Floor-level uplighting accentuating concrete texture
- No decorative fixtures — functional track, recessed only
- Neon tube or LED strip as sole color element

---

## Textiles & Patterns
- Zero pattern — texture only
- Heavy, dark-toned velvet on sofas
- Unfinished canvas or linen
- Cowhide or hair-on-hide rug (contrast element)
- Chunky wool throw in charcoal or ecru

---

## Architectural Elements
- Raw in-situ concrete walls (board-form pattern visible)
- Cantilevered staircases with exposed concrete slab
- Slot windows creating blade-of-light effects
- Double-height spaces with no mezzanine (monolithic mass)
- Concrete ceiling with exposed services in grid
- Window walls of glass curtain set within concrete frame

---

## Contemporary Brutalism (2024–2025)
- Warmer concrete mixes (buff, sandy aggregate)
- Plants — large-scale sculptural species (monstera, fiddle leaf)
- Art objects as single warmth counterpoint
- Limewash and plaster imitating concrete for residential scale
- Curved concrete forms softening hard-angle history
- Brutalist + Japandi fusion emerging as a high-design sub-style

---

## Room Applications
- **Living:** Board-formed concrete walls, concrete slab coffee table, dark velvet sofa, single large artwork, slot window light shaft
- **Bedroom:** Concrete headboard panel, dark-stained oak floor, single concrete pendant, minimal steel-frame bed
- **Kitchen:** Polished concrete worktop, black rubber floor, matte black fixtures, open slab shelf
- **Bathroom:** Poured concrete basin, board-formed walls, frosted slot window, zinc accessories
