# Industrial Interior Design

---

## Philosophy
Structural honesty. Expose what is usually hidden — the pipes, the ducts, the brick, the beams. Beauty is inherent in function, in the traces of making, in the scale of production. Industrial design was born in New York's SoHo in the late 1960s when artists colonized abandoned factories, unable to afford to finish them. What began as necessity became the most influential interior aesthetic of the late 20th century. The 2024–2025 "Soft Industrial" evolution brings warmth, plants, leather and linen to the raw structure without erasing its character.

---

## Core Characteristics
1. Exposed structural elements — brick, steel, concrete, ductwork, pipes
2. Open-plan with double or triple-height ceilings (minimum 3.5m)
3. Oversized steel-framed windows (Crittal-style) with black profiles
4. Polished or sealed concrete floors
5. Exposed Edison filament or globe pendant lighting on conduit
6. Furniture that contrasts with rawness — leather, velvet, warm wood
7. Minimal interior walls — function zones defined by furniture arrangement
8. Reclaimed materials — factory salvage, railway sleepers, scaffold boards
9. Dark metal palette — matte black, gun metal, oxidized steel
10. Oversized, architectural scale — small-scale objects disappear
11. Art displayed as in a gallery — single large works, bare walls
12. Urban views through large glass; no curtains or minimal industrial shutters

---

## Three Variations

### NYC Loft (Original)
Raw, unfiltered; artists' live-work studios; exposed everything; scale and emptiness as design language. The uncompromised version.

### Converted Warehouse
Larger scale; retained industrial equipment; massive windows; high ceilings; more space to zone and furnish without losing character.

### Soft Industrial (2024–2025)
Raw structure + warm materials; plants; leather and linen softening; artisan ceramics alongside factory objects. The commercially successful evolution.

## Materials
- **Exposed brick (London stock or NY red)** — feature walls, structural
- **Polished or ground concrete** — flooring throughout
- **Cast iron columns and beams** — structural; celebrated, not concealed
- **Reclaimed scaffold boards** — shelving, furniture tops
- **Railway sleeper (oak or pine)** — dining tables, benches
- **Matte black steel** — frames, shelving, pendants, door hardware
- **Distressed leather** — sofas, chairs (breaking-in period appreciated)
- **Galvanized steel ducting** — HVAC exposed as design element
- **Plate glass (oversized)** — windows, partitions, splashbacks
- **Weathered copper pipe** — plumbing exposed; pendants; curtain rails
- **Machine-pressed metal (perforated or punched)** — shelving panels, room dividers
- **Old-growth reclaimed timber** — feature walls, bespoke furniture

## Signature Furniture
- Chesterfield sofa in aged saddle leather (contrasting with raw walls)
- Factory roller cart repurposed as coffee table
- Metal school/industrial chairs (Tolix chair, zinc-coated, Xavière Pauchard 1934)
- Reclaimed timber dining table on steel trestle legs
- Wire mesh locker room storage unit
- Metal-framed Edison-lamp floor lamp
- Vintage factory clock as oversized wall art

---

## Lighting
- Edison filament bulbs (ST64, G125) in vintage cage holders on conduit
- Industrial pendant clusters on conduit rails
- Factory-style explosion-proof pendant (enamel hemisphere shade)
- Neon sign art as ambient lighting
- Track lighting on steel rail for gallery lighting
- Large Crittal-style windows maximizing daylight (no soft furnishings on glass)

---

## Textiles & Patterns
- Aged, cracked leather upholstery
- Canvas and cotton duck cloth
- Minimal pattern — matte solid textures preferred
- Persian or Oriental rug as contrasting warmth element
- Herringbone tweed on accent chairs (soft industrial)
- Linen in raw, undyed state

---

## Architectural Elements
- Exposed brick walls (all or feature)
- Steel I-beam columns and lintels
- Factory multi-pane windows (Crittal, double-height)
- Concrete floor with visible aggregate or polish
- Open ceiling with exposed services (HVAC, conduit, pipes)
- Mezzanine sleeping level with steel guardrail

---

## Soft Industrial (2024–2025)
- Warm terracotta plaster over brick softens rawness
- Trailing plants and large-format indoor trees
- Linen and bouclé replacing vinyl and naugahyde
- Artisan ceramics alongside factory objects
- Warm lighting (2700K candle-equivalent) replacing cold industrial whites
- Curved furniture introducing organic tension against raw geometry

---

## Room Applications
- **Living:** Exposed brick, concrete floor, leather Chesterfield, Persian rug, Tolix chairs, Edison pendants on conduit
- **Bedroom:** Mezzanine platform (steel guardrail), industrial shelving headboard, concrete feature wall, factory pendant
- **Kitchen:** Open steel shelving, concrete worktop, matte black hardware, exposed brick splashback, factory pendant
- **Bathroom:** Exposed pipe, concrete basin, subway tile, black iron fixtures, galvanized metal accessories
