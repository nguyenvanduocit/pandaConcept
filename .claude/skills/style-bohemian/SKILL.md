# Bohemian Interior Design

---

## Philosophy
Freedom from convention. Bohemian design is anti-rule — it invites the collector, the traveler, the romantic, the artist. Origins in 19th-century Paris bohemia: artists and intellectuals rejecting bourgeois norms, living amid layered, colorful, globally-sourced objects. Modern boho evolved through the 1960s counterculture and 1970s natural materials movement, then was refined into "boho-chic" in the 2000s and "modern bohemian" in the 2010s–2020s. The 2024–2025 iteration is more edited, more sustainable, and more culturally considered.

---

## Core Characteristics
1. Layered textiles — multiple rugs, blankets, cushions in varied patterns
2. Global objects — Moroccan lanterns, Indian block-print, Turkish kilim, African kente
3. Plants as structural design elements
4. Macramé and woven wall hangings
5. Vintage and antique mixed with handmade
6. Candles and low, warm light creating intimate atmosphere
7. Natural materials — rattan, bamboo, wood, jute, cotton, leather
8. Books and art on display everywhere
9. Floor cushions and low seating (Moroccan tradition)
10. Free-spirited arrangement — asymmetry celebrated
11. Earthy palette with jewel color pops
12. DIY and handmade objects given prominence alongside bought pieces

---

## Three Variations

### Traditional Boho
Maximalist, deeply layered; vintage market sourcing; traveling spirit; all patterns welcome. The original expression — zero rules applied.

### Modern Boho (2024–2025)
Cleaner lines; restrained palette; natural materials; less cluttered; sustainability-conscious. "Edited boho" — intention replaces accumulation.

### Boho-Chic
Mid-point; luxury materials + bohemian spirit; Anthropologie aesthetic; curated effortlessness. The commercial version that made the style mainstream.

## Materials
- **Rattan (natural cane and core)** — chairs, headboards, screens, pendant lights
- **Macramé (cotton cord)** — wall hangings, plant holders, room dividers
- **Kilim (flat-weave Turkish/Moroccan rug)** — flooring layer
- **Vintage teak or pine** — furniture, flooring
- **Cotton velvet (aged)** — cushions, window seat covers
- **Leather (weathered)** — poufs, floor seating
- **Wicker** — baskets, hampers, trays
- **Embroidered fabric (Indian mirror work)** — cushion covers, wall hangings
- **Hand-blown glass** — pendant lights, vases
- **Copper and brass (tarnished)** — lanterns, pots, fixtures
- **Driftwood** — frames, shelf brackets, wall art
- **Terracotta (handmade ceramic)** — pots, tableware, floor tile

## Signature Furniture
- Rattan peacock chair (iconic boho throne)
- Moroccan floor cushions (*pouf* ottomans)
- Carved wooden cabinet (Indian or Moroccan)
- Woven hammock or hanging rattan chair
- Low wooden coffee table with kilim layer
- Vintage trunk as coffee table or storage

---

## Lighting
- Moroccan lanterns (punched metal, colored glass inserts)
- Rattan pendant lights
- Candles — extensive, in varied holders (pillar, taper, votive)
- Edison bulbs in hanging clusters
- Colored glass votives creating ambient color wash
- Salt lamp as warm accent

---

## Textiles & Patterns
- Ikat — Southeast Asian resist-dye pattern (geometric or floral)
- Block print (Indian) — hand-stamped repeat on cotton
- Kilim — Turkish/Moroccan flat-weave geometric
- Suzani — Central Asian embroidered textile (large medallion pattern)
- Macramé — knotted cotton cord
- Crochet — granny-square or medallion pattern
- Tassel and fringe on cushions, rugs

---

## Architectural Elements
- Arched doorways and windows
- Brick or exposed plaster walls (whitewash or natural)
- Mosaic tile floors (Moroccan zellige)
- Low ceilings with draped fabric
- Window seat with cushion and floor cushions beneath

---

## Room Applications
- **Living:** Terracotta walls, rattan peacock chair, layered kilim rugs, Moroccan lantern, floor cushions, trailing plants
- **Bedroom:** Canopy bed with draped fabric, mixed textile bedding, macramé wall hanging, rattan pendant
- **Kitchen:** Open shelf with ceramics, terracotta tile, block-print curtain, woven basket storage
- **Bathroom:** Mosaic tile floor, terracotta vessel, plants, Moroccan mirror, woven basket

---

## 2024–2025 Modern Boho Specifics
- Cleaner base palette — warm white walls, terracotta, olive
- Sustainability: vintage/thrifted over new
- Natural dye textiles — indigo, madder, turmeric
- Less clutter, more intention — "edited boho"
- Global sourcing with cultural sensitivity and transparency
- Boho-biophilic crossover — plants elevated from decorative to structural
