# Biophilic Interior Design

---

## Philosophy
Reconnect with nature. Biophilia (E.O. Wilson, 1984) — the innate human need for connection to living systems — is backed by decades of research: nature-proximity reduces cortisol by 20%, improves cognitive function, and promotes healing. Biophilic design is not "adding plants" — it is a systematic approach to activating our biological response to the natural world through material, light, pattern, sound, and air. Every surface, every fixture, and every space arrangement is evaluated by how it connects the occupant to living systems.

---

## Core Characteristics
1. Living plant systems — not decorative; structural and abundant
2. Natural light maximization — circadian rhythm support
3. Views to nature — preserved or created (green walls, water views)
4. Natural materials exclusively — no petroleum-derived surfaces visible
5. Natural ventilation — operable windows, cross-flow, air movement
6. Water features — reflecting pool, indoor waterfall, rainfall shower
7. Organic forms — curved, irregular, biomorphic (nature has no right angles)
8. Natural textures — bark, stone, moss, wood grain, sand
9. Multi-sensory design — scent (plants, wood, earth), sound (water, wind, birds), touch
10. Prospect and refuge balance — open views from sheltered enclosure
11. Seasonal and temporal variation — allow natural light and temperature to shift
12. Living walls (*jardin vertical*) — full vertical plant systems

---

## Materials
- **Live-edge hardwood (walnut, elm, oak)** — furniture, shelving, feature walls
- **Craggy limestone / travertine** — floors, walls (texture variation)
- **Cork (natural, unprocessed)** — floors, walls, acoustic panels
- **Moss panel (preserved or living)** — feature wall system
- **Bamboo (culm or strand-woven)** — flooring, cladding, furniture
- **Rammed earth** — walls, feature panels
- **Natural stone (quartzite, schist, basalt)** — feature walls, bathroom
- **Woven grass (sisal, seagrass, jute)** — floor coverings, ceiling detail
- **Untreated linen and hemp** — window coverings, upholstery
- **Volcanic basalt (lava stone)** — sinks, surface tile
- **Reclaimed hardwood (visible history)** — floors, cladding
- **Clay and earthenware (unglazed)** — tableware, planters

## Plant Selection by Room
- **Living room:** Monstera deliciosa, Fiddle-leaf fig, Bird of Paradise, large specimen olive tree
- **Bedroom:** Peace lily (air purifying), Snake plant, Aloe vera, Lavender (pot)
- **Kitchen:** Herb garden wall (basil, thyme, rosemary), Pothos trailing from shelf
- **Bathroom:** Fern (humidity-loving), Orchid, Moss wall, Air plants (Tillandsia)

---

## Living Wall Systems
- Felt pocket systems (Woolly Pocket) — lightweight, modular
- Modular hydroponics (Naava, Greenworks) — automated irrigation
- Preserved moss panels — no maintenance, zero water requirement
- Trough planting at sill height — traditional and accessible

---

## Water Feature Integration
- Indoor reflecting pool (10–15cm depth sufficient for effect)
- Rainfall shower with pebble floor (bathroom as restorative space)
- Wall-mounted water cascade (stainless or stone)
- Tabletop fountain (seasonal, portable)

---

## Signature Furniture
- Live-edge walnut or elm dining table on steel hairpin legs
- Cork-seat stool cluster (natural, unfinished)
- Rattan lounge chair with linen cushion
- Stone bench (carved basalt or limestone)
- Reclaimed timber platform bed
- Woven seagrass or sisal ottoman

---

## Lighting
- Full-spectrum circadian lighting systems (tunable, shifts with daylight)
- Skylights and sun tunnels maximizing daylight
- Solar tubes bringing daylight to interior rooms
- Morning: cool blue-white (6500K) — energizing
- Evening: warm amber (2700K) — melatonin-supportive
- Avoid overhead blue-white (fluorescent or cool LED) entirely

---

## Textiles & Patterns
- Botanical print (hand-drawn leaf, not graphic)
- Abstract organic forms (pebble, branch, growth ring)
- Undyed and raw fibers — no synthetic dyes
- Jute and sisal rugs — natural underfoot texture
- Moss-embroidered cushion covers

---

## Architectural Elements
- Green roof or planted terrace visible from interior
- Floor-to-ceiling operable glass system to garden
- Internal courtyard with planting (Japanese garden reference)
- Skylights over plant zones
- Living wall as room divider

---

## Scientific Metrics
- Plants: Minimum 1 plant per 9 sq m for air quality benefit (NASA study)
- Views: 30-minute nature view exposure reduces stress markers measurably
- Materials: Natural surfaces trigger parasympathetic nervous system response
- Daylight: Minimum 300 lux at desk height for cognitive function

---

## Room Applications
- **Living:** Floor-to-ceiling glass to garden, live-edge dining, moss wall panel, rattan seating, linen curtains, monstera specimen
- **Bedroom:** Snake plant + peace lily, cork floor, linen bedding, skylight, no artificial overhead light at night
- **Kitchen:** Herb garden wall, live-edge shelf, stone basin, bamboo accessories, natural ventilation window
- **Bathroom:** Preserved moss wall, pebble floor rain shower, teak bench, orchid display, volcanic stone basin

---

## 2024–2025 Trends
- Data-driven biophilic design — sensors for air quality, light levels, humidity
- Biophilic + technology integration (smart irrigation, automated grow lights)
- Fermentation and food-growing integrated (kombucha corner, herb garden)
- Mental health-focused spaces (*restorative design* as architectural brief)
- Sound design — water sounds, birdsong channels, white noise systems
