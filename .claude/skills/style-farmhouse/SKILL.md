# Farmhouse Interior Design

---

## Philosophy
The practical beauty of agricultural life elevated to domestic ideal. Farmhouse design honors the history of the working farm — the functional kitchen at the center, the wide-plank floor, the apron sink — while layering comfort and personality. Modern farmhouse (2015–2025 Joanna Gaines era) cleaned up and lightened the palette; now evolving toward warmer, more colorful "new farmhouse." The farmhouse home is generous, lived-in, and never pretentious.

---

## Core Characteristics
1. Apron-front (farmhouse) sink as kitchen centerpiece
2. Open shelving displaying functional objects (jars, crockery, baskets)
3. Shiplap wall cladding — painted white or natural
4. Exposed wooden beams on ceilings
5. Wide-plank hardwood floors — rough-sawn or wire-brushed
6. Matte black hardware throughout (modern evolution)
7. Barn doors on sliding hardware
8. Vintage or antique pieces mixed with new
9. White-painted furniture (*milk paint* heritage)
10. Galvanized steel accents — buckets, light fixtures, pipes
11. Mason jar accessory styling
12. Layered textiles — grain sack patterns, linen, gingham, buffalo check

---

## Two Variations

### Traditional Farmhouse
Authentic working-farm heritage; cluttered with purpose; darker stained wood; practical-first; cast iron; painted pine; quilts and rag rugs.

### Modern Farmhouse (2024–2025 Evolution)
Clean, bright; shiplap + open shelving; matte black hardware; updated with sage green cabinetry, curved edges, global accent pieces. The Joanna Gaines aesthetic refined and warmed.

## Materials
- **White oak (wire-brushed)** — flooring, cabinetry
- **Reclaimed pine / hemlock** — shiplap, furniture, ceiling planks
- **Fireclay (apron sink)** — kitchen sink material
- **Soapstone** — countertops (traditional farmhouse)
- **Quartz (white with grey veining)** — modern farmhouse countertop
- **Galvanized steel** — light pendants, hardware, shelving brackets
- **Cast iron** — cookware, clawfoot tub, hooks, doorstops
- **Cotton (grain sack print)** — cushions, chair seats, market bags
- **Wool plaid** — throw blankets, accent textile
- **Milk-painted wood** — furniture, cabinetry in chalk-white or cream
- **Brick (whitewashed)** — kitchen backsplash, feature wall
- **Limestone or concrete tile** — floor (modern farmhouse)

## Architectural Elements
- Shiplap wall cladding (horizontal white planks)
- Exposed ceiling beams (painted white in modern, stained dark in traditional)
- Barn door on sliding track hardware
- Open-plan kitchen-living with island as divider
- Built-in mudroom with hooks, bench, cubbies
- Board-and-batten exterior siding

---

## Room Applications
- **Kitchen:** Apron sink, open shelving with ceramic display, shiplap ceiling, black iron pendants, sage green or white cabinetry
- **Living:** Shiplap feature wall, linen sofa, galvanized metal side table, layered grain sack and buffalo check textiles
- **Bedroom:** Linen or quilt bedding, reclaimed wood headboard, sliding barn door, vintage lamp
- **Bathroom:** Clawfoot tub, shiplap walls, black iron fixtures, mason jar storage, subway tile

---

## 2024–2025 "New Farmhouse" Evolution
- Warm, earthy tones replacing all-white (sage, clay, dusty rose)
- Curved edges on cabinetry replacing sharp square
- Natural and reclaimed materials emphasized
- Integration of global accent pieces (Moroccan tile, Japanese ceramic)
- "Modern Cottage" as adjacent trend
- Sage green becoming the signature 2024–2025 farmhouse color

## The Farmhouse Kitchen: Element by Element

### The Apron Sink
- Also called: farmhouse sink, Belfast sink (UK)
- Material: fireclay (most durable), cast iron (heavy, needs reinforced cabinet), composite
- Sizes: 24", 30", 33", 36" — 33" most common single-bowl
- Brands: Rohl, Shaw's (UK original), Kohler, Kraus
- Mount: undermount preferred for clean look; top-mount for traditional

### Open Shelving
- Material: reclaimed pine, white oak, painted MDF, scaffold board
- Bracket: black iron (modern farmhouse), raw steel, hand-forged wrought iron
- Display logic: white ceramics at back, coloured objects middle, plants at ends
- Spacing: 35–40cm between shelves; items should breathe

### The Barn Door
- Track hardware: black steel, exposed on wall above doorway
- Door material: reclaimed barn board (original), shiplap panels, Z-brace construction
- Sizing: door must be 5–10cm wider than opening on each side
- Brands: Calhome, Barn Door Hardware Store, custom blacksmiths

## Shiplap vs. Board-and-Batten vs. Beadboard

| Material | Profile | Best Application | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shiplap | Horizontal overlap reveal | Feature walls, ceilings | Clean, modern farmhouse |
| Board-and-batten | Vertical boards + narrow cover strip | Exterior, mudroom | Traditional, vertical emphasis |
| Beadboard | Narrow vertical grooves | Wainscoting, bathroom | Cottage, vintage |
| Tongue-and-groove | Interlocking horizontal | Ceiling, floor | Casual, rustic |

## Joanna Gaines Design Principles (Modern Farmhouse Reference)
The HGTV Fixer Upper aesthetic (2013–2018) defined a generation of American farmhouse design:
1. Start with white shiplap — it brightens and unifies
2. Anchor with a statement piece (antique door, vintage sign, industrial clock)
3. Layer with texture: linen, jute, galvanized metal, wood grain
4. Add warmth through plants: eucalyptus, olive, cotton stems
5. Mix old and new: vintage wooden tray + new white ceramics
6. Black hardware unifies: door knobs, faucets, light fixtures — all matte black

## 2024–2025 New Farmhouse Colour Evolution
- **Sage green cabinets** — replacing all-white; Benjamin Moore HC-117 Pale Sage, Sherwin-Williams Sage
- **Dusty rose accents** — soft, warm, nostalgic; cushions, ceramics
- **Warm clay walls** — replacing stark white; Sherwin-Williams Accessible Beige, Antique White
- **Natural wood tones** — blonde oak replacing painted white wood in flooring
- **Terracotta tile** — replacing grey slate in mudrooms and kitchens
